A mini archive about the rise and influence of spanish
language rock in Latin America and Los Angeles

Coded and written by Nate Steckel

Part of what lead to Spanish language rocks rise in popularity was actually the suppression of American and British rock music by the
military Junta who controlled Argentina at the time. Eventually they banned all english language music, which directed people's attentions
to spanish language rock groups like Soda Stereo and helped shift their musical tastes. For more information about Soda Stero, their
influence, and similar bands, click here. For information about the archive creator and his work, click here.
